Background:
These two designs are mockups of a Heads-Up Display for a hypothetical horror game. The main gameplay loop involves traversing an enclosed enviroment and completing certain tasks (styalized as "imperfections") while avoiding enemies. After completing all tasks in the level, the exit opens and all enemies immedietly begin to close in on you, requiring you to think fast and run for your life.

Role:
I am the sole creator of these designs. No AI was used in the making of the works. Human creativity and ingenuity can never be replaced.

Design Process:
Each of the HUD elements are kept relatively simple and non-intrusive to avoid distracting the player and allowing them to quickly identify and recognize the information conveyed to them. To the bottom left, basic shapes associated with health and stamina (i.e. a heart and a lightning bolt) were placed next to their respective meters, which lets players easily identify which meter the statistic belongs to. At the bottom right, the player's current inventory is displayed, which holds any consumable items they may have picked up. Finally, at the top of the screen, the number of unfinished tasks is displayed, letting players know how close they are to completing a level at a glance.

Due to the panic-inducing nature of the escape sequence, the HUD changes to reflect the current tone. During the escape, the HUD elements become a deep red as the objectives bar becomes a pleading warning for the player to run for their life. Furthermore, the HUD is overlayed with static, as if the very HUD itself is breaking down while all hell breaks loose around you. These effects combined aims to further urge the player to make their escape, as the corrupted HUD implies that not even the fourth wall will save them anymore
